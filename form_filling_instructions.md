# FDA MedWatch Form Filling Instructions

## Official Form Completion Guidelines

### General Instructions

1. **Complete All Applicable Sections**
   - Only fill sections relevant to your specific report
   - Leave inapplicable sections blank
   - Do not force data into irrelevant fields

2. **Date Format Requirements**
   - **REQUIRED FORMAT:** DD-MMM-YYYY
   - Examples:
     - October 12, 2019 = 12-OCT-2019
     - January 5, 2024 = 05-JAN-2024
     - March 30, 2025 = 30-MAR-2025
   - If exact date is unknown, provide best estimate (see Section B3)

3. **Narrative Entry Guidelines**
   - If fields don't provide adequate space, attach additional pages
   - When attaching pages:
     - Identify all pages as "Page __ of __"
     - Indicate appropriate Section and block number next to continuation
     - Include phrase "continued" at end of field with additional information

4. **Multiple Products/Devices**
   - **If reporting >2 suspect products:**
     - Complete full Form FDA 3500 for first two products
     - Attach additional Form FDA 3500 with ONLY Section D filled for each extra product
   - **If reporting >1 suspect medical device:**
     - Complete full Form FDA 3500 for one device
     - Attach additional Form FDA 3500 with ONLY Section E filled for each extra device

5. **Section D (Suspect Products) Applies To:**
   - Drugs (prescription and OTC)
   - Biologics
   - Cosmetics
   - Human cells, tissues, and cellular/tissue-based products (HCT/Ps)
   - Whole blood or blood components (plasma, platelets, red blood cells)

6. **Device User Facility Reporting**
   - If serious adverse event with device occurred in device user facility (NOT doctor's office)
   - Facility may be legally required to report to FDA and/or manufacturer
   - Report to facility directly OR notify person who handles such reporting

## Section-by-Section Instructions

### **Section A: Patient Information**

#### A1. Patient Identifier
- Do NOT use patient name or other identifying information
- Use initials, assigned case number, or de-identified token
- Example: "PT-001" or "Patient_Token_12345"

#### A2. Age at Time of Event
- Enter in years, months, or days
- Specify unit (e.g., "45 years", "6 months", "2 days")

#### A3. Sex
- Male
- Female
- Unknown

#### A4. Weight
- Include units (lbs or kg)
- Example: "165 lbs" or "75 kg"

---

### **Section B: Adverse Event or Product Problem**

#### B1. Adverse Event or Product Problem
**Check all that apply:**
- Adverse Event
- Product Problem (e.g., defects/malfunctions)

**If Product Problem:**
- Product Use Error
- Problem with Different Manufacturer of Same Medicine

#### B2. Outcomes Attributed to Adverse Event
**Check all that apply:**
- Death (enter date: DD-MMM-YYYY)
- Life-threatening
- Hospitalization - initial or prolonged
- Disability or permanent damage
- Congenital anomaly/birth defect
- Required intervention to prevent permanent impairment/damage
- Other serious (important medical event)

#### B3. Date of Event
- Format: DD-MMM-YYYY
- If unknown, provide best estimate and note "approximate"

#### B4. Date of This Report
- Format: DD-MMM-YYYY
- Enter date you are submitting this report

#### B5. Describe Event or Problem
**Narrative field - Include:**
- Timeline of events
- Symptoms and severity
- Treatment administered
- Clinical course
- Lab/diagnostic results
- Outcome
- Any relevant context

**Tips:**
- Be clear and chronological
- Use medical terminology appropriately
- Include specific vital signs, lab values if available
- Note "continued" if using additional pages

---

### **Section C: Suspect Medication(s)**

#### For EACH suspect medication, provide:

**C1. Name**
- Generic name and/or brand name
- Example: "Lisinopril (Prinivil)"

**C2. Dose, Frequency & Route Used**
- Dose: Amount per administration
- Frequency: How often (daily, BID, PRN, etc.)
- Route: Oral, IV, IM, topical, etc.
- Example: "10mg, once daily, oral"

**C3. Therapy Dates**
- Start date: DD-MMM-YYYY
- Stop date: DD-MMM-YYYY (if applicable)

**C4. Diagnosis for Use (Indication)**
- Why medication was prescribed
- Example: "Hypertension"

**C5. Event Abated After Use Stopped or Dose Reduced?**
- Yes
- No
- Doesn't apply

**C6. Lot # (if known)**
- From medication packaging

**C7. Exp. Date (if known)**
- Format: DD-MMM-YYYY

**C8. Event Reappeared After Reintroduction?**
- Yes
- No
- Doesn't apply

**C9. NDC # (if known)**
- National Drug Code (11 digits)
- From medication packaging

**C10. Concomitant Medical Products**
- List ALL other medications patient was taking
- Include supplements, OTC drugs
- Format same as C1-C4

---

### **Section D: Suspect Product(s)**

**Use this section for drugs, biologics, cosmetics, HCT/Ps, blood products**

#### D1. Name, Strength, Manufacturer/Compounder
- Full product name
- Strength/concentration
- Manufacturer name
- Example: "Influenza Vaccine, Quadrivalent - Sanofi Pasteur"

#### D2. Dose or Amount
- Amount per administration
- Example: "0.5 mL"

#### D3. Frequency
- How often administered
- Example: "Single dose"

#### D4. Route
- Method of administration
- Examples: IM, IV, PO, topical, subcutaneous

#### D5. Dosage Form
- Tablet, capsule, injection, cream, etc.

#### D6. Therapy Dates
- Start: DD-MMM-YYYY
- Stop: DD-MMM-YYYY

#### D7. Diagnosis/Indication for Use
- Why product was used
- Example: "Seasonal influenza prophylaxis"

#### D8. Event Abated After Stopping/Reducing?
- Yes / No / Doesn't apply

#### D9. Lot #
- From product packaging
- CRITICAL for vaccines and biologics

#### D10. Expiration Date
- Format: DD-MMM-YYYY

#### D11. Event Reappeared After Reintroduction?
- Yes / No / Doesn't apply

#### D12. NDC # or Unique ID
- For tracking and identification

---

### **Section E: Suspect Medical Device**

**Complete if event involves medical device**

#### E1. Brand Name
- Manufacturer's brand name

#### E2. Type of Device
- Generic category

#### E3. Manufacturer Name & Address
- Full contact information

#### E4. Model #, Catalog #, Serial #, Lot #
- All identifying numbers from device

#### E5. Operator of Device
- Health professional
- Lay user/patient
- Other

#### E6. Expiration Date
- Format: DD-MMM-YYYY

#### E7. If Implanted, Give Date
- Format: DD-MMM-YYYY

#### E8. If Explanted, Give Date
- Format: DD-MMM-YYYY

#### E9. Device Available for Evaluation?
- Yes / No / Returned to manufacturer

#### E10. Concomitant Medical Products
- Other devices used at same time

---

### **Section F: Other (Describe)**

**Use for additional relevant information:**
- Medical history relevant to event
- Previous adverse reactions
- Family history if relevant
- Environmental factors
- Patient's baseline health status

---

### **Section G: Reporter Information**

#### G1. Name & Address
- Reporter's name (person filling form)
- Full mailing address
- **Required for FDA follow-up**

#### G2. Health Professional?
- Yes / No
- Determines reporting category

#### G3. Occupation
- Physician, nurse, pharmacist, etc.

#### G4. Initial Reporter
- Also reported to manufacturer? Yes/No
- Also reported to user facility? Yes/No

#### G5. If you do NOT want your identity disclosed to the manufacturer, check here
- Privacy option

#### G6. Date of This Report
- Format: DD-MMM-YYYY

## Special Situations

### Vaccine Adverse Events
- **ALSO report to VAERS** (Vaccine Adverse Event Reporting System)
- Use https://vaers.hhs.gov/esub/index.jsp
- Both FDA MedWatch AND VAERS reporting may be appropriate

### Blood Product Reactions
- Report transfusion reactions
- Include blood type, compatibility testing results
- Note time from transfusion to reaction

### Device Malfunctions
- Save device if possible
- Take photos of defect/malfunction
- Include serial numbers, lot numbers

### Cosmetic Reactions
- Include specific product name and manufacturer
- Note all ingredients if known
- Include where product was purchased

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ **Incorrect date format** (use DD-MMM-YYYY, not MM/DD/YYYY)
❌ **Using patient identifying information** (use tokens/initials)
❌ **Incomplete narratives** (be thorough in Section B5)
❌ **Missing lot numbers** (especially critical for vaccines)
❌ **Forgetting concomitant medications**
❌ **Not checking outcome boxes** in Section B2
❌ **Leaving reporter information incomplete**

## Submission Checklist

Before submitting, verify:

✅ All applicable sections completed
✅ Dates in DD-MMM-YYYY format
✅ Patient identifiers de-identified
✅ Narrative is clear and complete
✅ Outcomes checked in Section B2
✅ Product lot numbers included (if available)
✅ Concomitant medications listed
✅ Reporter information complete
✅ Additional pages labeled if used
✅ Form reviewed for accuracy

## For VigilantAI Autonomous Filling

When using AI agent to fill form:

1. **Provide data in this structure:**
   - Section A fields
   - Section B fields (outcomes, dates, narrative)
   - Section C/D fields (product info)
   - Section F fields (medical history)
   - Section G fields (reporter)

2. **Agent must handle:**
   - Date format conversion to DD-MMM-YYYY
   - Multi-page navigation
   - Checkbox selections
   - Dropdown menus
   - Text area entries

3. **Safety gate:**
   - Agent must STOP before final Submit button
   - Human review required before submission
   - Screenshot of completed form for verification
